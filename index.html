Haiku For Two

Toshi loved the noodle girl. He didn’t just like her. He LOVED her. Under the cherry blossoms, he watches her stall from afar, day after day.

The summer heat, the cramped coffin apartment, his meager bento. He cannot afford even a bowl of her noodles. He dreams of taking her on a swan boat, of one day speaking with her. But in a world mediated by screens, how does one approach?

"Poetry Love or Hate"

A national phenomenon. Each day at 3 PM, one poet can find true love—or heartbreak—live before millions. Toshi knows this is his chance. He must submit a haiku before time runs out.

Live on the Air

Mrs. Gamshaw ambushes the noodle stall. Cameras everywhere. Toshi steps forward, trembling.

Toshi reads:

"You are noodle girl
I am no one you should know
I feel for you LOVE"

The noodle girl is revealed to be part of the cart—mechanical, not human. Wires and gears where a heart should be.

The Aftermath

Toshi’s heartbreak is national entertainment. The crowd disperses. Tomorrow, another poet. Another story. Toshi is left alone, emptiness where love once dwelled.

A quiet emptiness. The stump is empty. The noodle cart is gone. Only the memory remains.

End of Experience.
